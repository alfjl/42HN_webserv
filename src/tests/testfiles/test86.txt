      them at higher (640 x 480) resolution with a code that identifies

      the Fractal of the Day on your Web page, you may do so provided
      you mention that it is from Sprott's Fractal Gallery and provide a
      link back to this page. If you want to make your own fractals, I

    <p><img src="redball.gif"> The following fractals are standard Julia
      sets of the function <i>z</i>^2 + <i>c</i>. They were produced
      automatically by the <a href="software.htm">program</a> <a
        href="software/julia256.exe">julia256.exe</a> by <a

      plane for interesting cases. The names consist of two four-digit
      hexadecimal numbers <i>p</i> and <i>q</i> such that <i>c</i> is
      given by <i>c</i> = -2 + <i>p</i> / 21845 +<i> i q</i> / 43691.
      The plots cover the range <i>z</i> = (-0.02, 0.02) + (-0.02,
      0.02)<i> i</i>. </p>

    <p><img src="redball.gif"> The following icons are produced from 3-D
      strange attractors by mapping the <i>x</i>-coordinate to radius
      and the <i>y</i>-coordinate to angle and replicating the pattern
      with different orientations. The <i>z</i>-coordinate is
      represented by one of 256 colors, and a shadow is added to enhance
      the illusion of depth. The technique is described in a paper <a
        href="pubs/paper219.htm">"Strange Attractor Symmetric Icons"</a>
      by <a href="sprott.htm">J. C. Sprott</a>. The equations used are
      coded into the name according to a scheme described in the book <i><a
          href="sa.htm">Strange Attractors: Creating Patterns in Chaos</a></i>
      by <a href="sprott.htm">Julien C. Sprott</a>. Additional such
      cases can be produced automatically by the <a href="software.htm">program</

      <a href="software/icon256.exe">icon256.exe</a>. If you like these,
      you can view an <a href="fractals/icons/">index</a> of 100
      additional such examples, or an <a href="fractals/symmetry/">index</a>
      of cyclic symmetric attractor anaglyphs produced by a different
      method. You can also view an <a href="fractals/tilings/">index</a>
